Vršac Tower

The Vršac Tower is a fortress located on Vršac Hill above the town of Vršac, Serbia. It was built by Despot Đurađ Branković in the mid-15th century for defense against the Ottoman Empire. Today, the tower is restored and open to visitors, offering a spectacular view of the town and surrounding area.

Town Hall

The Town Hall in Vršac (Magistrate) was built in 1795 and is one of the most beautiful Baroque buildings in this part of Serbia. Today it serves as the seat of local government and a symbol of the city's administration and history.

Roman Catholic Church

The Roman Catholic Church of St. Gerard of Sagredo in Vršac was built in 1863. It is one of the largest Catholic cathedrals in Serbia, known for its stained glass windows and Neo-Gothic style. Located in the town center, it is an important religious and cultural landmark.

Bishop's Palace

The Bishop’s Palace in Vršac is the residence of the Bishop of Banat. It was built in the 18th century and is one of the most important Orthodox residences in Serbia. It is known for its beauty, library, and iconostasis.

Romanian Church

The Romanian Orthodox Church in Vršac was built in 1913 and is located in the very center of the town. It serves as the religious center of the Romanian community in Vršac and is recognizable for its domes and richly decorated interior.
